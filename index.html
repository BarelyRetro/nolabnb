New Orleans Occupancy vs Airbnb

Authored by: Robert Early

New Orleans is in an era of demographic change. The city saw a large drop off in population, particularly after the devastation of Hurrican Katrina in 2005. The first map on this page shows changes in occupied housing units in New Orleans neighborhoods from 2000 to 2010 according to the US Census. Most of the area saw a decrease in occupancy over the decade. According to the City of New Orleans , it ranks in the top ten nationally for homelessness with a rate of 302.07 per 100,000 residents.

The second map shows Airbnb listings from 2020. Although Airbnb launched in 2008, the company did not officially operate in New Orleans until 2016. The city did impose stricter rules on hosts in 2019. Most of Airbnb's are centered around the main business and tourism sectors (primarily the French Quarter and Central Business District) around the city center. However the neighborhoods around that area make up the middle range of both maps, meaning a loss of occupancy from 2000 but also a significant number of Airbnb properties.

While Airbnb does provide a source of supplemental income for some people who rely on it, it also consumes a lot of the available housing in New Orleans as people purchase investment properties. According to this 2020 data, 61 hosts have 10 or more listings. Those 61 make up less than two percent of hosts and average 23 listings each, accounting for over 1400 listings.

The results of the 2020 census will be interesting to compare to this data.
